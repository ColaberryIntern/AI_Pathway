Chapter Two

Why AI skips its homework

Three concepts that explain chain-of-thought prompting — and why it changes everything for complex work.

I
The Prediction Problem
Language models don't "think" — they predict the most statistically likely next token. Without a structured pathway, they jump to plausible-sounding conclusions that skip real reasoning steps. This is why direct questions about complex topics produce confident but shallow answers.
II
The Chain Mechanism
Chain-of-thought prompting forces the model to generate intermediate reasoning steps before concluding. Each generated step becomes part of the input for the next token — so reasoning literally improves its own quality as it runs. You're not adding guardrails; you're changing the computation itself.
III
The Marketing Payoff
For B2B marketers, CoT is the difference between getting a generic campaign outline and getting a structured brief that cites your ICP data, accounts for competitive positioning, and recommends channels based on your actual past performance — in a single interaction.
The anatomy of a chain-of-thought prompt
1
Define the problem space & context
2
Analyze each factor independently
3
Cross-reference and synthesize
4
Produce a structured output
Chapter Three

The technique, in practice

Two real scenarios from your work. Same skill, applied differently.

The situation
Q3 product launch for a new AI analytics feature. You have ICP data, past campaign performance, and competitor positioning. You need a complete brief — normally a 2-hour exercise.
📄 Q2-campaign-results.csv
📄 ICP-profile-2024.pdf
📄 competitor-matrix.xlsx
Your Chain-of-Thought Prompt
I'm launching a B2B SaaS analytics feature in Q3. Work through this step by step: Step 1 — Audience: Review the attached ICP profile. Who is the primary buyer? What pain points does this feature address most acutely? Step 2 — Competitive context: Using the competitor matrix, identify our key differentiation. What gap are we filling that competitors leave open? Step 3 — Channel strategy: Based on Q2 results, which channels drove highest-quality pipeline? Explain your reasoning before recommending. Step 4 — Synthesis: Combine your analysis into a structured brief with: positioning statement, 3 messaging pillars, recommended channel mix, and Q3 KPIs. Show your reasoning at each step before moving to the next.
Field Note
The phrase "show your reasoning at each step before moving to the next" is the critical CoT instruction. Without it, the model synthesizes immediately and skips the analytical grounding that makes the brief trustworthy.
The situation
Your VP wants a competitive intelligence memo before Monday's board meeting. You have G2 review exports, an analyst report, and competitor website notes. You need a clear strategic recommendation.
📄 g2-reviews-export.csv
📄 gartner-report-2025.pdf
📄 competitor-websites.txt
Your Chain-of-Thought Prompt
Competitive analysis for an executive memo. Build on each step before proceeding: Step 1 — Customer sentiment: From the G2 reviews, what are the top 3 complaints about each competitor? Quantify where possible. Step 2 — Analyst framing: How does the Gartner report characterize the market? Where does it see white space? Step 3 — Gap triangulation: Cross-reference Steps 1 and 2. Which unmet needs appear in both customer complaints AND analyst white space? Step 4 — Executive memo: Write a 3-paragraph strategic recommendation capturing the identified gap. Cite your step-by-step reasoning inline.
Field Note
Cross-referencing two data sources in Step 3 is an advanced CoT pattern called triangulation. The "cite your reasoning inline" instruction in Step 4 creates an auditable output — executives can trace every claim back to evidence.
Chapter Four

Building your CoT agent

Configure a reusable agent that applies chain-of-thought reasoning automatically. Complete each field; your agent spec builds as you write.

1
The agent's primary role
State the specific marketing workflow this agent will handle. Specificity makes the CoT chain more targeted.
2
The reasoning chain
Define the 3–5 steps the agent must work through before synthesizing. Each step should build on the previous.
3
Required inputs
What documents must always be provided to ground the reasoning?
4
Output format
What structure should the final synthesized output follow?
Your Agent Specification
agent "marketing-cot-agent" {
  role: "Campaign brief generator for B2B SaaS product launches"

  chain_of_thought: "Step 1: Audience & pain point analysis → Step 2: Competitive differentiation → Step 3: Channel fit → Step 4: Brief synthesis"

  requires: ["ICP profile, past campaign performance data, competitor matrix"]

  output_format: "Positioning statement · 3 messaging pillars · Channel mix · Q-KPIs"

  // System instruction appended to all prompts:
  system_cot: "Think step by step. Show reasoning at each stage before synthesizing."
}
