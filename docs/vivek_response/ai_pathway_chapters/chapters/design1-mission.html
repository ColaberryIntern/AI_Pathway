Section 02 — AI Concepts

How chain-of-thought actually works

Most people give AI the question and expect the answer. CoT changes the contract: you make the AI show its work before concluding. Reasoning quality jumps.

🧠
The Problem
LLMs predict the next token. Without guidance, they skip steps and hallucinate conclusions — especially on multi-variable problems like campaign analysis.
🔗
The Mechanism
CoT forces the model to generate intermediate reasoning steps. Each step conditions the next token — so the path to the answer improves its own quality.
📈
The Payoff
Google Research found CoT improved large-model accuracy on complex tasks by 40–60%. For you: fewer revisions, more reliable strategic outputs.
The Chain: How a CoT prompt executes
STEP 1
Define the problem space & constraints
STEP 2
Analyze each factor independently
STEP 3
Synthesize across factors
STEP 4
Produce structured recommendation
Section 03 — Live Examples

CoT in your actual work

Scenario
Q3 product launch for your new AI-powered analytics feature. You have ICP data, past campaign performance, and competitor positioning. You need a complete campaign brief — normally a 2-hour exercise.
📄 Q2-campaign-results.csv
📄 ICP-profile-2024.pdf
📄 competitor-matrix.xlsx
Chain-of-Thought Prompt
I'm launching a B2B SaaS analytics feature in Q3. Work through this step by step: Step 1 — Audience: Review the attached ICP profile. Who is the primary buyer? What pain points does this feature address most acutely? Step 2 — Competitive context: Using the competitor matrix, identify our key differentiation. What gap are we filling that competitors leave open? Step 3 — Channel strategy: Based on Q2 campaign results, which channels drove highest-quality pipeline for similar features? Explain your reasoning before recommending. Step 4 — Brief synthesis: Now combine your analysis above into a structured campaign brief with: positioning statement, 3 messaging pillars, recommended channel mix, and Q3 KPIs. Show your reasoning at each step before moving to the next.
Why this works
Each step forces the model to reason from your specific data before synthesizing. The final brief is grounded in evidence you provided — not generic marketing advice. The phrase "show your reasoning before moving to the next" is the CoT trigger that prevents premature conclusions.
Scenario
Your VP wants a competitive intelligence memo before Monday's board meeting. You have 4 competitor websites, 3 G2 review exports, and 2 analyst reports. You need a clear strategic recommendation.
📄 g2-reviews-export.csv
📄 gartner-report-2025.pdf
📄 competitor-websites.txt
Chain-of-Thought Prompt
Competitive analysis for an executive memo. Think through each step, then build on your prior analysis: Step 1 — Customer sentiment: From the G2 reviews, what are the top 3 complaints about each competitor? Quantify where possible. Step 2 — Analyst positioning: How does the Gartner report characterize the market? Where does it see gaps? Step 3 — Gap mapping: Cross-reference Steps 1 and 2. Which unmet needs appear in both customer complaints AND analyst white space? Step 4 — Strategic recommendation: Based on your analysis, write a 3-paragraph executive memo recommending where we should invest to capture the identified gap. Cite your step-by-step reasoning inline.
Why this works
The cross-reference in Step 3 is advanced CoT technique — you're making the model triangulate across two evidence sources before concluding. The instruction to "cite your reasoning inline" in Step 4 keeps the output auditable for executives who will probe the claims.
Section 04 — Build Your Agent

Design your CoT marketing agent

Follow the steps to configure an agent that uses chain-of-thought reasoning for your specific marketing workflows. Watch the agent spec build in real time.

1
Define the agent's role
What is this agent's primary job? Be specific about the marketing workflow it will support.
2
Set reasoning depth
How many chain-of-thought steps should the agent work through? More steps = more reliable but slower.
3
Specify required inputs
What documents or data must the agent always receive to ground its reasoning?
4
Define the output format
What structure should the final synthesized output follow?
Agent Spec · Live Preview
agent "marketing-cot-agent" {
  # Role
  role: "Campaign brief generator for B2B SaaS product launches"

  # Reasoning chain
  chain_of_thought: [
    "audience → competitive context → channel fit → brief synthesis"
  ]

  # Required inputs
  requires: [
    "ICP profile PDF, past campaign CSV, competitor positioning doc"
  ]

  # Output schema
  output: {
    format: "Positioning statement, 3 messaging pillars, channel mix, Q-KPIs"
  }

  # CoT instruction appended to all prompts
  system_append: "Think step by step. Show reasoning at each stage before synthesizing."
}
