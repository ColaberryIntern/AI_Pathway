Consistent Chapter Structure
All five designs implement the same five-section curriculum — only the UX language differs.
01
Scenario & Objectives
Learner profile (role, industry, level), current state, desired A→B transformation, time commitment.
02
AI Concepts
3 core concepts with visual explanations. Mechanism, application, payoff. No jargon without definition.
03
Live Examples (×2)
Two real-work scenarios with attached documents, full prompt, and annotated breakdown of why it works.
04
Build Your Agent
Guided agent configuration form with live-updating spec. Learner customises to their actual workflow and leaves with a deployable artifact.
05
Completion & Next Steps
Skill confirmation, earned badges/XP (design-dependent), next recommended skills from the ontology.
Adult Learning Principles Applied
Each design applies a different emphasis across these evidence-based principles.
🎯
Goal Orientation
Every chapter opens with a clear A→B journey tied to the learner's actual role and workflow. Adults learn when they see direct relevance to their job.
🔨
Learning by Doing
The agent builder in Section 4 produces a real deployable artifact — not a quiz. Constructivist learning: build something you actually keep.
📖
Worked Examples
Two industry-specific examples with full prompts, attached documents, and expert annotation. Reduces cognitive load vs. abstract instruction.
🔓
Progressive Disclosure
Sections unlock sequentially — learners can't skip to the agent builder without context. Prevents overwhelm and ensures prerequisite knowledge.
🗂️
Spaced Retrieval
Concepts introduced in Section 2 are immediately applied in Section 3 examples and then transferred to Section 4 agent design. Three retrieval moments per concept.
🤝
Self-Determination
Learners customise the agent to their own workflow. Autonomy and personalisation are core SDT motivators — learners who input their own data engage more deeply.
